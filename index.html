Yesterday, Today and Tomorrow — A Complete Picture for the Thinking Mind

Every generation witnesses a turning point. From bullock carts to tractors. From handwritten letters to smartphones. Today, we stand at the edge of another revolution — Artificial Intelligence.

Unlike earlier technologies that changed how we work, AI is beginning to change how we think, decide, and create. It is already shaping medicine, governance, science, engineering, and business — and it is coming to our villages, our farms, and our homes.

In our village of Biranarasinghpur, we have doctors, IAS officers, scientists, engineers, and entrepreneurs. This article is for all of you — a clear, honest, and practical picture of AI, without jargon or hype.

"AI is neither magic nor a menace. It is a human-made tool — and human wisdom decides its impact."

01

How Did AI Begin?

The story of thinking machines — from a bold 1950s question to a global revolution

Early computers were like very strict clerks — they followed every instruction exactly, but could not learn anything new. Then, in the 1950s, scientists asked a bold question: "Can machines learn from experience, just like humans do?" That question gave birth to Artificial Intelligence.

For decades, progress was slow. But two things changed everything: the explosion of data from the internet, and vastly more powerful computers. By the 2010s, AI could recognise faces, understand speech, and beat world champions at chess and Go. Then, in November 2022, ChatGPT arrived — reaching 100 million users in just two months. Faster than any technology in human history.

The AI Journey — Key Milestones
🌾 Village Analogy

A child in our village learns farming not from a textbook, but by observing seasons, understanding the soil, and learning from elders. AI learns the same way — from data and experience. The more data it sees, the better it gets — just like the more seasons a farmer experiences, the wiser their judgement.

02

AI, GenAI & AGI — The Three Terms Explained

Understanding the difference between what exists, what creates, and what is yet to come

🤖
AI — Artificial Intelligence
Specialist

Does one task very well. Face unlock, spam filter, Google Maps rerouting. Each AI is trained for exactly one job and knows nothing beyond it.

✨
GenAI — Generative AI
Creator

Creates new content — text, images, code, music, video. ChatGPT writes a legal brief. DALL-E paints a picture. It doesn't just answer — it makes.

🌟
AGI — General Intelligence
Future Horizon

Thinks like a human across all domains. Does not exist yet. A long-term scientific goal requiring careful research and ethics.

Traditional AI is discriminative — it classifies, predicts, and detects. GenAI, on the other hand, creates. ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude are trained on essentially all the text humanity has ever written, generating responses at extraordinary scale.

What Generative AI Can Create
💡 Simple Analogy

AI is like a skilled plumber — extraordinary at one job. GenAI is like a talented artist who can paint, write poetry, compose music, and build furniture. AGI would be like a complete, fully capable human being — but one that does not yet exist.

04

How AI Is Transforming Our Professions

Our village is home to people from many distinguished professional walks. Here is what AI means for each of them.

AI Productivity Gains by Profession (Estimated)
For Doctors & Healthcare
Your AI Diagnostic Partner

AI can study an X-ray, retinal scan, or CT image and spot early signs of cancer, diabetic damage, or heart disease — often before a human eye would notice. Google's DeepMind identifies over 50 eye conditions with specialist-level accuracy. In Odisha, AI tools are helping ASHA workers conduct accurate preliminary diagnoses.

For senior doctors, GenAI handles cognitive burden — summarising a patient's history in seconds, generating differential diagnoses, flagging drug interactions. AlphaFold solved the 50-year protein folding mystery, opening new drug discovery pathways. AI gives every doctor a tireless, encyclopaedic second opinion.

For IAS / OAS Officers & Governance
AI and the Intelligent State

The MyGov AI helpdesk handled over 3 crore citizen interactions during COVID-19 — in multiple languages, around the clock. Kerala's AI flood prediction system saved thousands of lives in 2018 with advance warnings hours earlier than traditional systems.

AI-powered satellite imagery shows crop stress, encroachment, or construction progress without physical inspection. Andhra Pradesh's AI land records system reduced disputes dramatically. For the modern IAS or OAS officer, AI is the most powerful governance amplifier ever built.

For Scientists & Researchers
The Universal Research Accelerant

ISRO uses deep learning to analyse satellite images for crop monitoring and disaster assessment. Tools like Semantic Scholar summarise thousands of papers and identify research gaps. A PhD student who would spend six months on a literature review can now do it in a week.

In genomics, AI identifies disease-related genes across millions of data points. In materials science, it predicts properties of undiscovered materials. The pace of human scientific discovery is accelerating dramatically.

For Engineers & Technology
The AI Copilot in Every Workshop

GitHub Copilot writes code alongside engineers, catching errors in real time. Studies show 55% productivity gains. Civil engineers simulate earthquake responses and bridge wind loads — thousands of virtual tests in hours. Electrical engineers use AI for smart grid management and predictive maintenance.

In electronics and telecommunications — a domain I have worked in since my M.Sc. days — AI is enabling 5G/6G network optimisation too complex for manual control. Engineers who embrace AI as a partner will build the infrastructure of tomorrow.

For Entrepreneurs & Business Leaders
The Great Equaliser

A solo entrepreneur in Puri today can generate professional advertising copy, design a logo, analyse sales data, respond to customers in ten languages, and manage accounts — all using freely available AI tools at nearly zero cost. The capability gap between small and large businesses has largely closed.

Agricultural entrepreneurs use AI platforms like Kisan AI and Fasal for soil data, weather forecasts, and market price predictions. At Brandscapes Worldwide, I have seen directly how businesses deploying AI thoughtfully are pulling decisively ahead.

05

AI in Odisha — Closer Than You Think

AI is not just a Silicon Valley story. It is already entering our state, our fields, and our clinics.

AI Serving Odisha
Agriculture

AI recommends the right crop for your specific soil, predicts pest attacks before they spread, and tells farmers the optimal time to sell. Apps like Fasal and Kisan AI are already in use by Odisha farmers.

Disaster Management

Cyclone Fani (2019) caused far fewer casualties than historical norms, partly because AI-assisted systems gave authorities 72+ hours of accurate advance warning for mass evacuations.

Rural Healthcare

AI tools help ASHA workers screen for anaemia, tuberculosis, and maternal health risks — without specialist doctors in every block. Telemedicine platforms use AI to prioritise and route patients.

Odia Language AI

The government's Bhashini project is building voice and text AI in Odia. Citizens will soon access any government service by simply speaking in their own language.

06

Five Myths About AI — Busted

Fear and misunderstanding about AI are as common as excitement. Let us address them directly.

MYTH
REALITY
AI will take all our jobs
Jobs evolve, not disappear. Tractors helped farmers — they did not replace them.
AI thinks like a human brain
AI has no feelings, consciousness, or awareness. It calculates — it does not feel.
AI is always correct
AI makes mistakes, especially with rare situations. Always verify important outputs.
AI is dangerous like the movies
AI is a tool controlled entirely by humans. A hammer builds or breaks — your choice.
AI is only for cities
Bhashini, Kisan AI, and ASHA apps are already in use across rural India.

The most important myth to address: AI will make human expertise obsolete. The evidence strongly suggests the opposite — AI amplifies human expertise. A doctor with AI sees more patients and makes fewer errors. An IAS officer with AI analysis makes better-informed decisions.

🧭 The Questions for Our Generation

Not "Will AI replace us?" but "How do we use AI to help our farmers get better prices? How do we bring specialist doctors to every village? How do we ensure AI speaks Odia and not just English? How do we make sure the benefits of AI reach Biranarasinghpur and not just Bengaluru?" These are questions for people like us to answer.

Conclusion: From Fear to Understanding

AI is not magic. It is not the Terminator. It is the latest — and most powerful — expression of human ingenuity: our drive to build tools that extend what we can do, see, know, and create.

For Biranarasinghpur — a village that has always produced thinkers, leaders, and people of consequence — the call is not merely to use AI, but to understand it, shape it, and guide it toward the good of all. Not just the privileged few in cities, but the farmer in the field, the patient in the village clinic, and the citizen who speaks only Odia.

"The machines are learning. The only question is — are we learning too?"
